Our Objectives

ASAAL is a group formed to represent the people. We want to help South Asians get a strong voice in our local and federal governments, by learning about and spreading awareness for our issues and supporting politicians who will help us do something about them. Our group is made up of several supporters, South Asian and not, and is allied with different unions and activist groups across New York and the nation. Together, we can all strive for a better world, one step at a time.

Education

Education is one of the most valuable things that any person will ever have. With education, we can do our jobs better and live more desirably. However, many immigrants, including South Asians, do not know about the labor laws and benefits that unions and their bosses can get for them. This often leads to new immigrant workers being taken advantage of and exploited, since they work hard but do not understand how unfair of a reward they are getting. ASAAL makes it our duty to educate people about what they are supposed to be recieving and how they can get it.

Activism

ASAAL would not be able to do much if it was not contantly fighting for its people. We take pride in standing up for ourselves and others in times of need. The South Asian and labor communities in general need a voice when they are subjected to injustice. We also make sure we help out with civil issues in other areas as well, such as immigration reform and police violence cases. It is our duty to help bring an end to these issues, and we are very proud of it. Take a stand for universal justice!

Participation

We believe that being an active member in our communities in very important to helping each other and solving problems. ASAAL members are proud to volunteer in political campaigns, rallies, and information distribution so that we can all work towards a greater tomorrow. Door knocking, advocacy, job fairs, training; those are just a few tasks on our large to-do list every year! Do you have what it takes? Join ASAAL and become a part of the team!
